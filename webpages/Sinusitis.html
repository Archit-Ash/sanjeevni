Sinusitis

Sinusitis is an inflammation or swelling of the tissue lining the sinuses. Sinuses are small, air-filled cavities located inside the skull, which are connected to the nose. Sinusitis can be acute (short-term) or chronic (long-term). Acute sinusitis usually develops after a cold or other upper respiratory tract infection and may last up to four weeks. Chronic sinusitis lasts for at least 12 weeks and can be caused by a variety of factors, including nasal polyps, deviated septum, and allergies. Symptoms of sinusitis include nasal congestion, sinus pressure or pain, thick nasal discharge, and fever. Treatment options include over-the-counter medications, antibiotics, and in some cases, surgery.

Home Remedies

  • Steam inhalation: Inhaling steam can help to loosen mucus and reduce congestion. You can do this by adding a few drops of eucalyptus oil to a bowl of hot water, covering your head with a towel, and breathing in the steam.
  • Nasal irrigation: Using a saline solution to rinse out the nasal passages can help to clear mucus and reduce inflammation. You can make your own saline solution by mixing 1/4 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda in 8 ounces of warm water.
  • Warm compresses: Applying a warm compress to the face can help to reduce sinus pressure and pain. You can do this by soaking a washcloth in warm water and applying it to the affected area.
  • Hydration: Drinking plenty of water and other fluids can help to thin mucus and promote sinus drainage.
  • Rest: Getting plenty of rest can help to boost the immune system and promote healing.
  • Spicy food: Eating spicy food may help to open up the nasal passages and relieve congestion.
  • Herbal tea: Drinking herbal teas such as ginger tea, turmeric tea, and thyme tea may help to relieve sinus congestion and inflammation.
