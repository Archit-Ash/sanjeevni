Home Remedies

  • Eating iron-rich foods: Foods that are high in iron include red meat, poultry, fish, beans, leafy green vegetables, and dried fruit. Eating these foods can help to increase the body's iron stores.
  • Eating foods that are high in vitamin C: Vitamin C helps the body to absorb iron more efficiently. Foods that are high in vitamin C include oranges, strawberries, kiwi, bell peppers, and tomatoes.
  • Eating foods that are high in folate: Folate helps the body to produce new red blood cells. Foods that are high in folate include leafy green vegetables, oranges, and beans.
  • Drinking beet juice: Beet juice is rich in iron and can help to increase the production of red blood cells.
  • Taking herbal supplements: Herbs such as alfalfa, yellow dock, and dandelion can help to increase the body's iron stores.
  • Consuming food made with iron-rich flours: Mixing iron-rich flour like jowar, bajra, ragi with wheat flour can enhance the iron content in the food consumed.
  • Drinking nettle tea: Nettle is high in iron, and drinking a cup of nettle tea a day can help to increase the body's iron stores.
